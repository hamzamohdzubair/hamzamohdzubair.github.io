Introduction¶

I like to summarize the entire Data Analysis / Data Science cycle with three words:

  • Question
  • Data
  • Insight

In fact this is the process of scientific inquiry that has been used and established since centuries by scientists and inverstigators in all kinds of fields. The question is a sort of prelude and the insight is the end-knowledge or conclusion of the process.

The most important and time consuming process in this three step cycle therefore is Data. There are four important things that you need to do with it to come up with an insight:

  1. Getting it
  2. Readying it
  3. Playing with it
  4. Diving in it

Each of these steps is a science in itself. The process of getting the data includes things like sampling techniques,etc. The process of readying the data for analysis is called Data Cleaning or Data Munging. There are a whole bunch of techniques, algorithms, packages developed entirely for this purpose itself. Playing with data is usually called as Exploratory Data Analysis or EDA and is an entire process in itself. Diving in the data is usually the advanced level of Data Analysis used in either large corporate settings or advanced-level academic research. It involves machine learning, AI, etc.

So here it is, the entire Data Analysis life cycle simplified in six steps: Question, Getting Data, Readying it, Playing with it, Diving deep, and Knowledge generation. Though this process seems linear and most Data Analysis life cycle in most books are linear or cyclical (meaning the last step feeding into the first step). But most people who practice Data Science regularly know that like everything in life this process is never linear. And in most cases the first step is usually the fourth, i.e. playing with data.

In most practical cases both in the academia and industry, you play with a certain small set of data, you look carefully at a sample before even starting to ask the questions, and collecting data.

Turns out the fourth step is actually the most important and this is where most of the time is spent by a data scientist. Here in this post we will try to cover the entire Data Science Process.
